The Analog Trilogy

Discover the three foundational albums that defined the Trieste sound. Recorded entirely on magnetic tape.

Nebbia (1972)

The debut masterpiece. Pure analog signal paths and deep space echoes.

Sequence (1974)

Introducing rhythm and sequence. A journey to the sun through warm oscillations.

The Euclidean Sea (1978)

The final transmission. A monolithic exploration of geometry and sound. Our magnum opus.

DreamSequence wasn't just a band; it was a movement. In a Trieste caught between borders, they found freedom in the frequency between two notes. Every recording was a ritual, every concert an exploration of the unknown.
